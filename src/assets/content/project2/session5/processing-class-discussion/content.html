Interquartile Range Notes

We have previously encountered measures of central tendency i.e. averages.

We are now interest in finding the spread of the distribution rather than a central value. We have various types of spread similar to having various types of average.

Range

The simplest measure to calculate is the range. This is simply the difference between the smallest and largest data values

Advantages: Easy to calculate, gives a general idea of the spread of a dataset

Disadvantages: Affected by outliers, values that are much smaller or larger than most of the other values, in a set of data

Inter-quartile Range

This is better than the range as we are only interested in the middle half of the data. We are therefore not as affected by outliers.

We first sort the data into ascending order and then circle the median (half way), lower quartile (one quarter way) and the upper quartile (three quarters way)
