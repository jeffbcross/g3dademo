TYPES OF GRAPHS AND CHARTS

Graphs and charts are a great way to communicate information visually.

When selecting the best way to present your data, think about the purpose of your graph or chart and what you are trying to convey

Should your data be shown as percentages, categories or frequencies?

Is your data categorical or continuous?

Categorical- grouped into non-overlapping categories

Continuous- measured on a scale or continuum

There are four main types of graphs and they can help us show different types of information: bar graphs, pie charts, line graphs, and histograms.

1. Bar Graph

Composed of separate bars that represent different categories of data

Useful for displaying categorical data

Best used to compare values across different categories

Have an X and Y axis

2. Pie Chart

Used to compare parts of a whole

Divided into sections that are equal in size to the quantity that is being represented

Can be helpful when data is represented with percentages

Used for displaying categorical data

3. Line Graph

Displays the relationship between two types of information

Useful in illustrating trends over time

Used for displaying categorical data

4. Histogram

Has connected bars that display the history or proportion of information that fall within the designated columns

Used for displaying continues data such as weight and test scores

Columns can be varying width

Guidelines for Formatting Graphs and Charts

Use a clear title for your chart or graph to convey the purpose

Use the axis titles to specify the unit of measurement

Label each part of the graph (this can be done using a legend if necessary)

Use different colors to help readers distinguish different categories

Keep it simple, do not distract the reader from the data you are presenting
